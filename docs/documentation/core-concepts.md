# Core Concepts

Almost everything in the Nimble system comes down to two kinds of thing: **things that live in your world** (characters and creatures) and **things you drag onto them** (classes, spells, equipment, and so on). Once that clicks, every sheet and pack in the system makes sense.

## Things that live in your world

These appear in the **Actors** sidebar tab and can be placed on the map as tokens. There are four kinds:

| Kind | Use it for |
| --- | --- |
| **Character** | Player heroes. The full sheet, with the works: ancestry, background, class, leveling, inventory, spells, mana, and hit dice. |
| **NPC** | The GM's standard creatures, meaning most monsters and NPCs. A lean stat-block-style sheet built for running fights, not for leveling. |
| **Minion** | Weak creatures fielded in numbers. Like NPCs, but built for swarms: the system can group minions together in combat and roll their attacks as a group. |
| **Solo Monster** | Boss-type creatures designed to fight a whole party alone, such as the legendary monsters. |

Everything in the **Nimble Monsters** pack is an NPC or a minion; everything in **Nimble Legendary Monsters** is a solo monster.

## Things you drag onto them

Everything else is an **item**, and in this system "item" means much more than physical gear. All of these are items you drag from a compendium (or the Items sidebar) onto a sheet:

- **Class** and **Subclass**
- **Ancestry** and **Background**
- **Boon**
- **Feature** (character abilities) and **Monster Feature** (creature abilities)
- **Spell**
- **Physical items**, the gear in your inventory

A class is not a menu you pick from. It's a thing you drag onto your character. Drop a class onto a character sheet and the sheet fills itself in: hit dice, saving throw tendencies, armor and weapon proficiencies, mana. As you level up, the class delivers its features to your sheet automatically, with the level-up window walking you through any choices. Ancestries, backgrounds, subclasses, and boons work the same way: drag them on, and what they provide arrives with them.

::: tip You usually won't drag these by hand
The character creator and level-up windows do the dragging for you. Knowing that it all works by drag-and-drop mostly matters when you homebrew or want to adjust a character manually.
:::

### The five kinds of physical item

Physical items (the system calls them "objects" in a few places) come in five flavors:

- **Weapon**: anything you attack with.
- **Armor**: what you wear.
- **Shield**: what you carry in your off hand.
- **Consumable**: potions, scrolls, and anything else that gets used up.
- **Miscellaneous**: everything else, such as rope, rations, trinkets, and loot.

The kind determines which fields the item sheet shows and how the item behaves in your inventory.

## Compendiums: the content library

The compendium packs are the system's read-only content library. Open a pack from the **Compendium Packs** sidebar tab to browse it, and drag anything out: onto a sheet, into the sidebar, or onto the map (for monsters).

::: warning Copy to your world before editing
Pack content is replaced whenever the system updates, so edits made inside a pack don't last. The habit to build: drag the thing into your world first (it becomes an independent copy), then edit the copy. Your world's copies are yours forever.
:::

## Items carry rules

Here's the idea that makes the whole system tick: an item can carry **rules**, small instructions that change the character carrying it. A cloak can raise your armor, a feature can add to your speed, a weapon can apply a condition when it hits, a class feature can set up a pool of dice for you to spend. When the item lands on a sheet, its rules take effect; remove the item and they're gone. Every class feature, boon, and magic item in the packs works this way, and you can write your own rules point-and-click with the [Rules Builder](rules-builder/index.md).
